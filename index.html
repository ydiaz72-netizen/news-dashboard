Today in the News

  • The attacker who rammed a truck into Temple Israel synagogue in West Bloomfield, Michigan, was identified as Ayman Mohamad Ghazali, a naturalised US citizen born in Lebanon who had lost four family members in a recent Israeli airstrike. The FBI is investigating the incident, and US Jewish communities called for heightened security nationwide.
Forward Look

What to Expect Next

  • Analysts expect continued escalation around the Strait of Hormuz, with Iran showing no sign of reopening the waterway; historically, disruptions to this chokepoint—through which roughly 20% of global oil passes—have triggered sharp and sustained increases in energy prices worldwide.
  • The deployment of additional US Marines and naval assets to the region suggests Washington may be preparing for expanded operations or a prolonged military posture; historically, such buildups in the Persian Gulf have preceded either intensified strikes or efforts to secure maritime corridors.
  • Iran's strikes on Gulf Arab states risk fracturing regional alignments; analysts expect Gulf capitals to intensify diplomatic efforts—potentially through intermediaries such as China or Turkey—to de-escalate, as occurred during previous Gulf tensions in 2019.
  • Global economic disruptions are likely to deepen in the coming days, with oil price surges already affecting fuel-importing nations such as Pakistan; food supply chains dependent on Persian Gulf shipping and fertiliser exports face further strain, according to industry leaders.
  • Security agencies in the US and Europe are expected to heighten vigilance around Jewish and other community institutions, following the Michigan synagogue attack and incidents in Amsterdam and Rotterdam, amid concerns that the widening conflict could inspire further lone-actor violence.

Middle East

Today in the News

  • The United States military struck military targets on Kharg Island, Iran's primary oil export hub, with President Trump stating forces had "totally obliterated" military sites and warning that Iran's oil infrastructure could be targeted next if Tehran continues to block the Strait of Hormuz. Iranian state media reported no damage to oil infrastructure.
  • All six crew members of a US military refuelling aircraft were confirmed dead after the plane crashed in western Iraq, bringing the total number of American troops killed in the Iran conflict to 13 since it began two weeks ago.
  • A French soldier was killed and six others wounded in a drone strike in Iraq's Kurdistan region, with President Macron calling the attack "unacceptable"; the soldiers were part of a Western military contingent conducting counter-jihadist operations.
  • Iran continued retaliatory strikes across the Gulf, targeting locations in Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and the UAE, where oil loading operations were reportedly suspended at a key port after an intercepted drone sparked a fire. Hamas publicly urged Iran to halt attacks on neighbouring Gulf states while affirming Tehran's right to self-defence against the US and Israel.
  • Israel intensified operations in Lebanon, carrying out airstrikes in central Beirut for the first time in this phase of the conflict, displacing hundreds of thousands, while also dropping leaflets over Beirut urging the disarming of Hezbollah. Israel separately announced it had killed top Iranian nuclear scientists in targeted strikes.
  • The US embassy in Baghdad was struck by a projectile on Saturday, with black smoke seen rising above the compound; separately, the Pentagon announced it would deploy the USS Tripoli amphibious assault ship and its Marine expeditionary unit from Japan to reinforce US forces in the Middle East.
Middle East — Forward Look

What to Expect Next

  • With Trump explicitly threatening Iran's oil infrastructure on Kharg Island — which handles roughly 90% of Iran's crude exports — analysts expect further escalation around the Strait of Hormuz, historically a flashpoint that, if disrupted, could remove a significant share of global oil supply and drive prices sharply higher.
  • Iran's expanding drone and missile strikes on Gulf state territory, and Hamas's unusual public call for Tehran to stop such attacks, suggest Gulf nations may face intensifying pressure to limit cooperation with US military operations; historically, GCC states have sought to avoid direct involvement in US-Iran confrontations to protect their own infrastructure.
  • The deployment of additional US amphibious forces from the Indo-Pacific to the Middle East is likely to fuel concern among US allies in Asia; analysts note this mirrors historical patterns where Middle East conflicts have diverted American military resources away from the Pacific, potentially benefiting China's strategic positioning.
  • With Vice President Vance notably distancing himself from the war and domestic discontent rising over surging fuel prices, historians and political analysts expect growing Congressional and public pressure on the administration to define an exit strategy — a dynamic that historically accompanied the early stages of the 2003 Iraq War.
  • Macron's offer to host Lebanon-Israel talks in Paris, combined with escalating Israeli strikes in Beirut, suggests European diplomatic efforts to contain the Lebanon front may accelerate in coming days, though historically such initiatives have required US backing to gain traction.
